## [Forge: Guardrails Boost 8B Model from 53% to 99% on Agentic Tasks](https://github.com/antoinezambelli/forge) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Antoine Zambelli released Forge, an open-source reliability layer that uses guardrails like retry nudges and error recovery to boost an 8B local model's accuracy on multi-step agentic tasks from 53% to 99.3%, matching frontier models like Claude Sonnet. This shows that with proper guardrails, small local models can rival expensive cloud APIs, making reliable agentic AI accessible on consumer hardware and reducing reliance on costly frontier models. Forge's guardrail stack includes five toggleable layers, with retry nudges and error recovery providing the largest accuracy gains; the serving backend also caused up to a 75-point accuracy swing, highlighting infrastructure's impact.

hackernews · zambelli · May 19, 12:23 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48192383)

**Background**: Agentic workflows involve LLMs making multi-step tool calls, where per-step errors compound. Guardrails are runtime controls that constrain model behavior to improve reliability. Forge addresses the 'compounding math problem' where 90% per-step accuracy yields only 40% success over 5 steps.
